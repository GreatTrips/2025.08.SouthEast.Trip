A 15-Day Southern Journey

Day 1: Tuesday, August 5, 2025

Arrival and Journey to the Capital

Your 15-day exploration of the American South begins this afternoon upon your arrival at Charleston International Airport. After securing your rental vehicle, your journey will commence not on the coast, but with an immediate two-hour drive inland to the state capital of South Carolina, Columbia. This initial leg of the trip takes you from the low-lying, humid coastal plain into the rolling hills of the state's interior, a transition that sets the stage for the diverse landscapes to come.

üìç First Stop: Quirky Public Art

A quick stop at the corner of Marion and Taylor Streets reveals the **World's Largest Fire Hydrant**, a sculpture titled "Busted Plug." At nearly 40 feet tall and weighing 675,000 pounds, this tilted, gushing sculpture by artist Blue Sky is a fun, unexpected landmark and a perfect, lighthearted photo op to officially mark the start of your travels.

Dossier: Columbia, South Carolina

Overview & Geography: The capital of South Carolina, Columbia is strategically situated on the Atlantic Seaboard Fall Line, where the hilly Piedmont region descends to the flat Coastal Plain. This location is defined by the confluence of the Saluda and Broad rivers, which merge here to form the Congaree River.

Etymology & Nicknames: Named in 1786 for Christopher Columbus, representing a poetic, New World identity. Common nicknames include "The Capital of Southern Hospitality" and "The Cola."

Day 2: Wednesday, August 6, 2025

Capital Exploration

Spend the day exploring Columbia's key sites. Start with a tour of the impressive **South Carolina State House**, noting the bronze stars that mark where Sherman's cannonballs struck during the Civil War. In the afternoon, visit the acclaimed **Riverbanks Zoo and Garden**, known for its natural habitat exhibits. Conclude the day with a relaxing stroll along the **Congaree Riverwalk**.

3

Day 3: Thursday, August 7, 2025

Return to the Lowcountry

Today you'll drive two hours back to the coast, arriving in the historic heart of the South: **Charleston**. After checking into your hotel in the historic district, take an evening stroll along The Battery, a fortified seawall, and admire the grand antebellum homes overlooking the harbor as the sun sets.

4

Day 4: Friday, August 8, 2025

Historic Charleston

Immerse yourself in American history with a morning ferry ride to **Fort Sumter**, where the first shots of the Civil War were fired. In the afternoon, wander through the charming streets south of Broad, photograph the iconic pastel-colored houses of **Rainbow Row**, and browse the stalls of the historic Charleston City Market.

Dossier: Charleston, South Carolina

Overview: Founded in 1670, Charleston is defined by its cobblestone streets, horse-drawn carriages, and pastel antebellum houses. It's known as the "Holy City" due to the prominence of church steeples on its low-rise skyline.

Architectural Influence: The city's unique "single house" architecture was designed to cope with the hot, humid climate, with long piazzas (porches) oriented to catch the prevailing sea breezes.

Day 5: Saturday, August 9, 2025

Journey to Savannah

A scenic two-hour drive south takes you across the border into Georgia and to the enchanting city of **Savannah**. After settling in, spend your afternoon in **Forsyth Park**, marveling at its iconic fountain and the Spanish moss-draped oak trees that define the city's atmosphere.

6

Day 6: Sunday, August 10, 2025

The Squares of Savannah

Discover Savannah's unique city plan by taking a walking or trolley tour of its 22 historic squares. Each square acts as a neighborhood park, rich with monuments and history. In the evening, explore the lively atmosphere and converted cotton warehouses of **River Street**, watching massive container ships glide by on the Savannah River.

Dossier: Savannah, Georgia

The Oglethorpe Plan: Savannah is America's first planned city. Its grid-like layout, known as the Oglethorpe Plan, was established in 1733 and is considered a masterpiece of urban planning.

A Haunted History: Often cited as one of America's most haunted cities, Savannah's history of wars, disease, and fires has led to countless ghost stories and a thriving ghost tour industry.

Day 7: Monday, August 11, 2025

Drive to the Modern South

Today involves a four-hour drive west to **Atlanta**, the sprawling capital of Georgia and a hub of the New South. The transition from the historic coast to this bustling metropolis is stark. Check into your hotel and spend the evening exploring the vibrant Midtown neighborhood.

8

Day 8: Tuesday, August 12, 2025

Atlanta's Icons

Visit Centennial Olympic Park, the legacy of the 1996 Olympic Games. Spend your day at two of Atlanta's most famous attractions located here: the massive **Georgia Aquarium**, one of the largest in the world, and the **World of Coca-Cola**, an interactive museum dedicated to the iconic beverage that was invented here.

Dossier: Atlanta, Georgia

A City Reborn: Completely burned during the Civil War, Atlanta's symbol is the Phoenix, a mythical bird that rises from the ashes. This represents its post-war reconstruction and its emergence as a national center of commerce.

Transportation Hub: Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is consistently one of the busiest airports in the world, making the city a critical gateway to the United States.

Day 9: Wednesday, August 13, 2025

On the Road to Music City

A four-hour drive northwest takes you into Tennessee and to its capital, **Nashville**, also known as "Music City." After checking in, dive headfirst into the city's culture with an evening on **Lower Broadway**, where live country music spills out of every honky-tonk bar.

10

Day 10: Thursday, August 14, 2025

The Sound of Nashville

Today is all about music history. Spend the morning at the **Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum**. In the afternoon, take a tour of the historic **Ryman Auditorium**, the "Mother Church of Country Music." If you can, catch a show at the Grand Ole Opry in the evening.

11

Day 11: Friday, August 15, 2025

Home of the Blues

Drive three hours west to **Memphis**, a city whose gritty soul has produced some of the most influential music in the world. Settle in and then head straight for **Beale Street**, the heart of the blues scene, for an evening of live music and world-famous barbecue.

12

Day 12: Saturday, August 16, 2025

The King and the Birth of Rock 'n' Roll

Make the pilgrimage to **Graceland**, the iconic home of Elvis Presley. Afterwards, visit **Sun Studio**, the legendary recording studio where Elvis, Johnny Cash, and Jerry Lee Lewis got their starts, and feel the history in the room where rock 'n' roll was born.

13

Day 13: Sunday, August 17, 2025

Down to the Bayou

A long six-hour drive south through the Mississippi Delta brings you to your final destination: **New Orleans, Louisiana**. The change in culture, cuisine, and architecture is immediate and profound. Check into your hotel in the **French Quarter** and spend your first evening soaking in the unique, vibrant atmosphere of Bourbon Street.
